Wireless Troubleshooting

If experiencing an inermittent issue, first replace the modem Identify all locations where Wi-Fi is used Put the puck in the location that most often uses Wi-Fi.

Use the puck for the following:

  1. Identify interference from other devices, optimum location of modem, and best channel to usel
  2. Run Optimizer test to determine best 802.11 network settings (A, B, G, N, AC) Routers on A and B network will cause problems. Recommend N or above.
  3. Verify RF level output of router (must be < 50 db)

AWG Settings:

  1. Lease time of 172,800 or greater
  2. Apple Products do not perform optimally on CH 7
  3. Lock the channel down on 1, 6, or 11
  4. Verify WMM is turned "ON"

Troubleshooting Intermittent And/Or Wi-Fi Issues

Before you begin (at least 2 minutes prior)

  1. Check signal history through Tech Mobile 2.0
  2. Check the HSD Trasmit, HSD Receive Power, HSD Signal to Noise, and HSD Uncorrectable Blocks levels of the MTA/Modem using Unified with a live poll
  3. HSD Tx (+30 to +50), HSD Rx(+10 to -10), HSD SNR(>32), HSD UB(=0)
  4. Use Meter, Wi-Fi Analyzer on mobile device, Wi-Fi Puck, to show speed customer

At the House

  1. With your meter verify DQJ (>7) on all DOCSIS frequencies (573 MHz-663 MHz KC)
  2. With your meter, run a complete DOCSIS scan and wait for pass/fail
  3. With your meter, perform a packet loss test; should be no packet loss
  4. Perform a speed test on all CPE to websites: speedtest.texas.rr.com & speedtest.net
  5. If you needed to troubleshoot a potential drop issue, connect a working test modem to the TAP

  • Keep your flashlight ON and with you at all times

  • Access
